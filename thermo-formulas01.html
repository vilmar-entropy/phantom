1.1 Mass and Weight
1) Weight, $F_g$
$F_g = mg$

where:
$m=$ mass
$g=$ gravitational acceleration

Note: You can click the formula name (e.g. Weight, $F_g$) to see the notations used in the formula.
1.2 Density, Specific Volume, Specific Weight
2) Density, $\rho$
$\rho =\frac{m}{V}$

where:
$m=$ mass
$V=$ total volume

3) Specific Volume, $\text{v}$
$\text{v} =\frac{V}{m} = \frac{1}{\rho}$

where:
$m=$ mass
$V=$ total volume
$\rho =$ density

4) Specific Weight, $\gamma$
$\gamma =\frac{F_g}{V} = \rho g$

where:
$F_g =$ weight
$V=$ total volume
$\rho =$ density
$g=$ gravitational acceleration

1.3 Pressure
5) Pressure due to Elevation, $P$
$P = \gamma h = \rho g h$

where:
$\gamma =$ specific weight
$h = $ the elevation where the pressure is measured
$\rho =$ density
$g=$ gravitational acceleration

6) Absolute Pressure, $P_{abs}$
$P_{abs} = P_{atm} + P_g$

where:
$ P_{atm} = $ atmospheric pressure
$ P_g = $ gage pressure

1.4 Temperature
7) Temperature in Fahrenheit, $t_F$
$t_F = \frac{9}{5} t_C + 32$

where:
$ t_C = $ temperature in Celsius

8) Temperature in Celsius, $t_C$
$t_C = \frac{5}{9}(t_F - 32)$

where:
$ t_F = $ temperature in Fahrenheit

1.5 Conservation of Mass
9) Law of Conservation of Mass
$[\text{mass entering}] - $ $ [\text{mass leaving}] = $ $[\text{change of mass stored in system }] $
10)Mass flow rate, $\dot m$
$ \dot m = \rho v A $

where:
$\rho = $ density
$ v = $ velocity of fluid
$ A = $ cross sectional area of the conduit where the fluid pass through

11) Continuity equation of Steady Flow
$[\text{mass entering}] = $ $ [\text{mass leaving}] $
$ \dot m = \rho _1 A_1 v_1 = \rho _2 A_2 v_2 $

where:
$\rho_1 = $ density of entering fluid
$ v_1 = $ velocity of entering fluid
$ A_1 = $ cross sectional area of the conduit where the fluid enters
$\rho_2 = $ density of leaving fluid
$ v_2 = $ velocity of leaving fluid
$ A_2 = $ cross sectional area of the conduit where the fluid leaves
