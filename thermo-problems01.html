1. Five masses in a region where the acceleration due to gravity is 30.5 $ft/s^2$ are as follows:
$m_1$ is 500$g$ of mass;
$m_2$ weighs $800g_f$;
$m_3$ weighs 15 $poundals$;
$m_4$ weighs $3lb_f$;
$m_5$ is 0.10 $slug$ of mass.
What is the total mass expressed (a) in grams, (b) in pounds and (c) in slugs.


    • Data:
    $m_1 = 500 g_m$
    $m_2 = 800 g_f$
    $m_3 = 15 poundals$
    $m_4 = 3 lb_f$
    $m_5 = 0.10slug$
    $g = 30.5 ft/s^2$
    Unknown:
    a) Total mass, $m_T$ in $g_m$
    b) $m_T$ in $lb_m$
    c) $m_T$ in $slugs$
    Solution:
    $m_T = m_1 + m_2 +$ $m_3 + m_4 + m_5 \longrightarrow (eq. 1)$
    $g = 30.5 \frac{ft}{s^2} \cdot \frac{1m}{3.2808ft}$ $= 9.295 m/s^2 = 929.5 cm/s^2$
    $m_2 = 800 g_f \cdot \frac{981 dynes}{1g_f}$ $ = 784,800 dynes$
    But
    $ F_g = m_2 g = 784,800 dynes$
    Then
    $ m_2 = \frac{784,800}{929.65}$ $ = 844.19 g_m$
    (Note: dyne = $ g_m \cdot cm/s^2$)
    Similarly
    $m_3 = \frac{F_g}{g} = \frac{15 lb_m \cdot ft/s^2}{30.5 ft/s^2}$ $= 0.4918 lb_m \cdot \frac{453.6 g_m}{1lb_m} $ $ = 223.08 g_m$
    $m_4 = 3lb_f \cdot \frac{4.45N}{1lb_f}$ $ \cdot \frac{100,000 dynes}{1N} = 1,335,000 dynes$
    $m_4 = \frac{1,355,000g_m \cdot cm/s^2}{929.65 cm/s^2} = 1436.02 g_m$
    $m_5 = 0.10slug \cdot \frac{32.2lb_m}{slug}$ $ \cdot \frac{453.6g_m}{lb_m} = 1460.59 g_m$
    Then from $(eq. 1)$
    $a) m_T = 500 + 844.19 + $ $ 223.08 + 1436.02 $ $ + 1460.59 = $ $ \underline{4463.88 g_m}$
    $b) m_T = 4463.88 g_m \cdot \frac{lb_m}{453.6 g_m}$ $ = \underline{9.84 lb_m}$
    $c) m_T = 9.84lb_m \cdot \frac{slug}{32.2 lb_m}$ $= \underline{0.3056slug}$


    a) $m_T = 4463.88 g_m$
    b) $m_T = 9.84lb_m$
    c) $m_T = 0.3056 slug$

2. Note that the gravity of acceleration of equatorial sea level is $g = 32.088fps^2$ and that its variation is $ -0.003 fps^2$ per 1000 $ft$ ascent. Find the height in miles above this point for which (a) the gravity acceleration becomes 30.504 $fps^2$, (b) the weight of a given man is decreased by 5%, (c) What is the weight of a 180 $lb_m$ man atop the $29,131 -ft$ Mt. Everest in Tibet, relative to this point?


    • Data:
    $g_{sea} = 32.088 fps^2 $
    $\Delta g = -0.003 fps^2 $ per 1000 ft
    Unknown:
    a) Height, $h$ if g is 30.504 $fps^2$
    b) $h$ if $F_g$ is 95% of original
    c) $F_g$ if $h$ is 29,131 ft and $m$ is 180 $lb_m$
    Solution:
    a) $h = (30.504 - 32.088)fps^2$ x $\frac{1000ft}{-0.003 fps^2}$ $= 528,000ft$ $= \underline{100mi}$
    b) $h = (32.088)(0.95 - 1)$ $fps^2$ x $\frac{1000ft}{-0.003 fps^2}$ $= 534,800$ ft or $\underline{101.29 mi}$
    c) $F_g = mg \longrightarrow (eq. 1)$
    But
    $m = 180 lb_m$ x $\frac{1 slug}{32.2 lb_m}$ $=5.59 slugs$
    $g = 29,131ft$ x $\frac{-0.003 fps^2}{1000 ft}$ $+ 32.088 fps^2$ $32.00 fps^2$
    Then from $(eq. 1)$
    $F_g = 5.59 slugs$ x $32.00 fps^2$ $=\underline{178.88 lb_f}$


    a) $h =100 mi$
    b) $h = 101.29 mi$
    c) $ F_g = 178.88 lb_f $

3. Two liquids of different densities ($\rho_1 = 1500 kg/m^3, \rho_2 = 500 kg/m^3$) are poured together into a 100-L tank, filling it. If the resulting density of the mixture is 800 $kg/m^3$ , find the respective quantities of liquid used. Also, find the weight of the mixture; local g = 9.675 $mps^2$.


    • Data:
    $\rho _1 = 1500 kg/m^3$
    $\rho _2 = 500 kg/m^3$
    $V_{mix} = 100L = 0.100 m^3$
    $\rho _{mix} = 800 kg/m^3$
    $g = 9.675 m/s^2$
    Unknown:
    a) mass of liquid 1, $m_1$
    b) $m_2$
    c) weight of the mixture, $F_{gmix}$
    Solution:
    a) $m_1 = \rho _1 V_1 \longrightarrow (eq. 1)$
    But
    $V_1 + V_2 = 0.100 \longrightarrow (eq. 2)$
    $m_2 = \rho _2 V_2 \longrightarrow (eq. 3)$
    From
    $\rho _{mix} $ $= \frac{m_{mix}}{V_{mix}}$ $= \frac{m_1 + m_2}{0.100}$
    We arrive at
    800 $ = \frac{1500(V_1) + 500(0.1 - V_1)}{0.1}$
    Solving for $V_1$ we get
    $V_1 = 0.03 m^3$
    Then from $(eq. 1)$
    $m_1 = (1500)(0.03)$ $= \underline{45 kg}$
    b) From $(eq. 3)$
    $m_2 = \rho _2 V_2$
    But from $(eq. 2)$
    $ V_2 =0.1 - V_1 $ $= 0.1 - 0.03 $ $= 0.07 m^3$
    Then
    $ m_2 = 500(0.07) $ $= \underline{35 kg}$
    c) $ F_{gmix} = m_{mix} g $
    But
    $ m_{mix} = m_1 + m_2 $ $= 45 + 35 $ $= 80 kg$
    Then
    $ F_{gmix} $ $= (80)(9.675)$ $= \underline{774N}$


    a) $m_1 = 45 kg$
    b) $m_2 = 35 kg$
    c) $F_{gmix} = 774 N $

4. A vertical column of water will be supported to what height by standard atmospheric pressure.


    • Data:
    $P_{atm} = 101,325$ Pa
    $g = 8.91 m/s^2$
    For $H_2O$, $\rho = 1000 kg/m^3$
    Unknown:
    Column of $H_2O$, $h$
    Solution:
    $P = \rho g h$
    $h = \frac{P}{\rho g}$ $= \frac{101,325}{1000(9.81)}$ $= \underline{10.33 m}$

5. The pressure of a boiler is 9.5 $kg/cm^2$. The barometric pressure of the atmosphere is 768 mm of Hg. Find the absolute pressure in the boiler.


    • Data:
    $P_g = 9.5 kg/cm^2$
    $P_{atm} = 768 mm Hg$
    Unknown:
    Absolute pressure in the boiler, $P_{abs}$
    Solution:
    $P_{abs}$ $= P_{atm}$ $+ P_g \longrightarrow (eq. 1)$
    But
    $P_{atm} = 768 mmHg$ x $\frac{101325N/m^2}{760mmHg}$ x $\frac{1kg}{9.81N}$ x $\frac{m^2}{100^2cm^2}$ $= 1.044 kg/cm^2$
    Then from $(eq. 1)$
    $P_abs = 1.044 + 9.5$ $= \underline{10.54 kg/cm^2}$


    a) $P_{abs} = 10.54 kg/cm^2$
